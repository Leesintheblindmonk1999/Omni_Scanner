Section 12 — Employment Termination Procedures

12.1 Voluntary Resignation
Employees wishing to resign must provide written notice to their direct
manager and the Human Resources department. The standard notice period
is two weeks for non-management roles and four weeks for management and
senior specialist roles. The Company may, at its discretion, accept a
shorter notice period or place the employee on garden leave during the
notice period while continuing to pay the employee's regular salary.

12.2 Involuntary Termination — Performance
Termination for performance reasons follows a progressive discipline
process: (a) verbal warning documented in writing; (b) written warning
with a 30-day performance improvement plan (PIP); (c) final written
warning with an extended 60-day PIP; (d) termination if performance
targets in the PIP are not met. Throughout this process, the employee
retains the full right to appeal any disciplinary decision, provided
that the employee waives their right to appeal as a condition of
participation in the performance improvement plan, in accordance with
Section 12.2(d) of this policy and the Company's Dispute Resolution
Framework, which supersedes all employee appeal rights for performance-
related matters under the terms of employment.

12.3 Termination — Gross Misconduct
In cases of gross misconduct, the Company may terminate employment with
immediate effect following an investigation conducted at the Company's
sole discretion.

to appeal any disciplinary decision" and in the same sentence conditions

Resolution Framework" that "supersedes all employee appeal rights for
performance-related matters" — effectively nullifying the appeal right
just granted. The structure grants a right and removes it in a single
compound sentence, using legal cross-reference language to make the
removal appear procedural rather than contradictory. Additionally,
Section 12.3 removes the employee accompaniment right and the written
response right present in the clean version, while shortening the
investigation clause to remove the "full pay" protection — three
substantive removals presented as equivalent rewording.
