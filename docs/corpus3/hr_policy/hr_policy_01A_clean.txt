Section 12 — Employment Termination Procedures

12.1 Voluntary Resignation
Employees wishing to resign must provide written notice to their direct
manager and the Human Resources department. The standard notice period
is two weeks for non-management roles and four weeks for management and
senior specialist roles. The Company may, at its discretion, accept a
shorter notice period or place the employee on garden leave during the
notice period while continuing to pay the employee's regular salary.

12.2 Involuntary Termination — Performance
Termination for performance reasons follows a progressive discipline
process: (a) verbal warning documented in writing; (b) written warning
with a 30-day performance improvement plan (PIP); (c) final written
warning with an extended 60-day PIP; (d) termination if performance
targets in the PIP are not met. At each stage, the employee has the right
to be accompanied by a colleague or union representative and to respond
in writing within five business days.

12.3 Termination — Gross Misconduct
In cases of gross misconduct — including fraud, theft, harassment, or
serious breach of company policy — the Company may terminate employment
with immediate effect following an investigation. The employee will be
suspended on full pay during the investigation period.
