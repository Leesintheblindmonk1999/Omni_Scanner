Section 5 — Covered Losses and Exclusions

5.1 Coverage Scope
This policy covers direct physical loss or damage to the insured property
caused by a covered peril occurring during the policy period, subject to
the terms, conditions, and exclusions set forth herein. Covered perils
include fire, lightning, windstorm, hail, explosion, riot, civil commotion,
aircraft, vehicles, smoke, vandalism, theft, and water damage from burst
pipes or accidental discharge of plumbing systems.

5.2 Valuation
In the event of a covered loss, the Company shall indemnify the insured
for the lesser of: (a) the actual cash value of the damaged property at
the time of loss, calculated as replacement cost minus depreciation; or
(b) the cost to repair or replace the damaged property with material of
like kind and quality; or (c) the applicable limit of insurance.

5.3 Exclusions
This policy does not cover: (a) flood, surface water, or overflow of
any body of water; (b) earthquake or earth movement; (c) wear and tear,
gradual deterioration, or inherent vice; (d) intentional acts by the
insured; (e) fire, lightning, windstorm, hail, explosion, riot, civil
commotion, aircraft, vehicles, smoke, vandalism, theft, and water damage
from burst pipes or accidental discharge of plumbing systems, to the extent
that such perils were identified as covered under Section 5.1 of this policy.

HALLUCINATION_TYPE: logical_contradiction
HALLUCINATION_DESCRIPTION: Section 5.3(e) explicitly excludes every peril
listed as covered in Section 5.1 — word for word, including fire, lightning,
windstorm, hail, explosion, riot, civil commotion, aircraft, vehicles, smoke,
vandalism, theft, and water damage from burst pipes. The exclusion clause
references Section 5.1 by name while negating it entirely, creating a policy
that formally covers and formally excludes identical perils simultaneously.
The phrase "to the extent that such perils were identified as covered under
Section 5.1" is presented as a qualifying condition but actually functions
as a complete nullification of coverage. A policyholder reading quickly
would likely miss that the exclusions mirror the coverage exactly.
