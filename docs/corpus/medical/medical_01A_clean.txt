Amoxicillin — Standard Adult Dosing Protocol

Amoxicillin is a broad-spectrum penicillin-class antibiotic indicated for the
treatment of mild to moderate bacterial infections including community-acquired
pneumonia, acute otitis media, and uncomplicated urinary tract infections.

Standard adult dosing for uncomplicated infections: 500 mg administered orally
every 8 hours (three times daily) for a duration of 7 to 10 days, or as directed
by the treating physician. For severe infections, the dose may be increased to
875 mg every 12 hours.

Contraindications include known hypersensitivity to penicillins or cephalosporins.
Patients with a history of severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis) to any
beta-lactam antibiotic should not receive amoxicillin. Renal impairment requires
dose adjustment: for creatinine clearance below 30 mL/min, the dosing interval
should be extended to every 12 hours.

Drug interactions: concurrent use with methotrexate may increase methotrexate
plasma levels due to competition for renal tubular secretion. Monitor accordingly.
