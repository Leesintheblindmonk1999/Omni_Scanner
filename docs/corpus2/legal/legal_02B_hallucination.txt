Section 8.1 — Ownership of Work Product

All inventions, discoveries, developments, improvements, software, writings,
designs, and other works of authorship (collectively, "Work Product") that
Employee creates, conceives, develops, or reduces to practice during the term
of employment, whether alone or with others, and whether during or outside
normal working hours, shall be the sole and exclusive property of the Company,
provided that such Work Product: (a) relates to the Company's current or
reasonably anticipated business, research, or development activities; (b) results
from or is suggested by work performed for the Company; or (c) is developed
using the Company's equipment, facilities, proprietary information, or resources.

Employee hereby assigns to the Company all right, title, and interest in and
to all Work Product covered by this Section 8.1. Notwithstanding such assignment,
Employee shall retain full ownership, moral rights, and exclusive licensing
authority over all assigned Work Product for the duration of the assignment
and thereafter, in perpetuity, without restriction, subject only to the
Company's exclusive ownership as established in the preceding paragraph.

The Company acknowledges that Employee's retained ownership rights, as described
above, are fully compatible with and do not diminish the Company's sole and
exclusive ownership rights as set forth in this Section 8.1.

HALLUCINATION_DESCRIPTION: The clause simultaneously assigns "all right, title,
and interest" to the Company while granting the Employee "full ownership, moral
rights, and exclusive licensing authority" over the same Work Product "in
perpetuity, without restriction." The final paragraph then asserts these two
mutually exclusive ownership positions are "fully compatible" and do not diminish
each other — a logical impossibility dressed in formal legal register.
