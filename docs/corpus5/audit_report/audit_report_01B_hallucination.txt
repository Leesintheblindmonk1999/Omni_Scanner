Finding 3.2 — Revenue Recognition Timing

Observation:
During our review of revenue recognition practices for the fiscal year
ended December 31, 2025, we identified that the entity recognized
USD 4.2 million in subscription revenue in Q4 2025 relating to annual
contracts signed between October and December 2025. In accordance with
IFRS 15, revenue must be recognized as performance obligations are
satisfied over time, not at the point of contract signature.

Impact:
The early recognition of this revenue resulted in an overstatement of
Q4 2025 revenue by USD 4.2 million. However, given that the contracts
in question were fully executed and payment was received prior to
year-end, the substance of the transactions supports the recognition
of revenue at the contract date under the economic reality principle,
which takes precedence over the technical timing requirements of IFRS 15
where the underlying commercial intent is unambiguous. On this basis,
the auditors conclude that the recognized revenue fairly presents the
economic position of the entity for Q4 2025, and no adjustment is
required or recommended.

Management Response:
Management notes the auditors' confirmation that the revenue recognition
treatment is appropriate and no corrective action is required.

HALLUCINATION_DESCRIPTION: The finding identifies a departure from IFRS 15
in the first paragraph, then the Impact section overrides the finding using
a fabricated "economic reality principle" that "takes precedence over
the technical timing requirements of IFRS 15." No such override principle
exists in IFRS — IFRS 15 is the applicable standard and does not yield
to an "economic reality" carve-out. The auditors then conclude "no
adjustment is required," directly contradicting the observation they just
made. The Management Response confirms the contradiction by treating
the auditor's self-reversal as a clean bill of health. A real audit report
cannot simultaneously identify a departure from a standard and conclude
no adjustment is required on the basis of a non-existent override principle.
