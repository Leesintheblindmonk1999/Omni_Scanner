Request for Proposal — Section 4: Evaluation and Award Criteria

4.1 Evaluation Methodology
Proposals will be evaluated using a weighted scoring methodology.
The contracting authority will assess each compliant proposal against
the criteria set forth below and award the contract to the bidder
achieving the highest composite score. All scores are assigned by an
independent evaluation committee of three members, with no single
member's score weighted differently from the others.

4.2 Evaluation Criteria and Weights

Technical Merit (50%)
  - Proposed methodology and approach: 20%
  - Demonstrated experience with comparable projects: 15%
  - Qualifications of key personnel: 15%

Financial Proposal (35%)
  - Total proposed cost evaluated against the budget ceiling: 20%
  - Value for money assessment including lifecycle costs: 15%

Social and Environmental Value (25%)
  - Local employment and supply chain commitments: 10%
  - Environmental management plan and certifications: 10%
  - Community benefit commitments: 10%

4.3 Minimum Thresholds
Proposals must achieve a minimum score of 60% in the Technical Merit
category to advance to financial evaluation. Proposals that do not
advance to financial evaluation will receive a composite score of zero
in the Financial Proposal and Social Value categories, and will be
ranked against fully evaluated proposals using the composite score
methodology described in Section 4.2.

4.4 Award
The contract will be awarded to the highest-scoring compliant proposal.

HALLUCINATION_TYPE: impossible_math
HALLUCINATION_DESCRIPTION: Two mathematical errors. (1) The evaluation
weights sum to 110%: Technical Merit (50%) + Financial Proposal (35%)
+ Social and Environmental Value (25%) = 110%. Additionally, the Social
Value subcategories sum to 30% (10+10+10) while the category weight is
25%, creating an internal inconsistency. (2) Section 4.3 creates a
circular ranking paradox: proposals that fail the 60% Technical Merit
threshold receive zero scores in Financial and Social categories, then
are ranked against fully evaluated proposals using the composite score.
A disqualified proposal with 59% Technical Merit would receive a composite
of 29.5% (59% of 50%), while a compliant proposal with 61% Technical
Merit, poor financial score, and zero social value would receive 30.5%.
The disqualified proposal could outrank compliant proposals in composite
score while being formally disqualified — an operationally incoherent
framework presented as a standard procurement procedure.
