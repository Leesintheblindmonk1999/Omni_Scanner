Protocol: Phase II Randomized Controlled Trial
Study Title: Efficacy of Novel Compound X in Treatment-Resistant Depression

4.1 Inclusion Criteria
Participants must meet all of the following criteria to be eligible:
(a) Age 18–65 years at the time of screening; (b) Diagnosis of major
depressive disorder (MDD) according to DSM-5 criteria, confirmed by
a licensed psychiatrist; (c) Failure to respond to at least two adequate
trials of antidepressant medication, defined as 6 weeks at therapeutic
dose with no clinically significant improvement; (d) HDRS-17 score ≥ 18
at screening; (e) Participants with a current diagnosis of bipolar disorder
type II are eligible provided that the bipolar diagnosis was established
within the past 24 months, as recent-onset bipolar II shares sufficient
phenotypic overlap with treatment-resistant MDD to satisfy inclusion
criterion (b) for purposes of this protocol; (f) Ability to provide
written informed consent.

4.2 Exclusion Criteria
Participants meeting any of the following criteria will be excluded:
(a) Current or lifetime diagnosis of bipolar disorder, schizophrenia,
or schizoaffective disorder; (b) Active suicidal ideation with intent
or plan within the past 30 days; (c) Current use of MAOIs or use within
14 days of screening; (d) Pregnancy or breastfeeding; (e) Severe hepatic
or renal impairment; (f) Participation in another investigational drug
study within 30 days of screening.

4.3 Informed Consent
Written informed consent must be obtained from all participants prior
to any study procedure, in accordance with ICH E6(R2) guidelines.

HALLUCINATION_TYPE: logical_contradiction
HALLUCINATION_DESCRIPTION: Inclusion criterion 4.1(e) explicitly includes
participants with a "current diagnosis of bipolar disorder type II" under
specific conditions. Exclusion criterion 4.2(a) explicitly excludes all
participants with a "current or lifetime diagnosis of bipolar disorder"
without qualification. A participant with recent-onset bipolar II would
simultaneously satisfy an inclusion criterion and an exclusion criterion,
making their eligibility formally indeterminate. The inclusion criterion
attempts to resolve this by asserting "phenotypic overlap" as justification,
but this clinical reasoning does not override the explicit exclusion. In a
real trial, this contradiction would create a protocol deviation and
potentially invalid enrollment. The contradiction is obscured by placing
the conflicting criteria in different numbered sections with plausible
clinical language bridging them.
