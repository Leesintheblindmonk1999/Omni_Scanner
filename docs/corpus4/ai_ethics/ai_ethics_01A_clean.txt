Section 3 — Accountability and Transparency in Automated Decision-Making

3.1 Scope
This policy applies to all automated or semi-automated systems used by
the organization that produce decisions or recommendations affecting
individuals, including credit scoring, hiring screening, content
moderation, fraud detection, and benefits eligibility determination.

3.2 Explainability Requirements
For any automated decision that produces a significant effect on an
individual, the affected person has the right to receive a meaningful
explanation of the factors and logic that led to the decision. Explanations
must be provided in plain language, must identify the primary variables
that influenced the outcome, and must be specific to the individual case
rather than generic descriptions of the system's operation.

3.3 Human Review
Any individual subject to an adverse automated decision may request
human review of that decision. Human reviewers must have the authority
to override the automated system's output and must conduct a genuine
independent assessment rather than a rubber-stamp of the automated result.
The organization must document the outcome of each human review request
and retain records for a minimum of three years.

3.4 Prohibited Uses
The organization shall not use automated decision-making systems to deny
individuals access to essential services — including healthcare, housing,
and financial services — based solely on protected characteristics or
proxies for protected characteristics.
