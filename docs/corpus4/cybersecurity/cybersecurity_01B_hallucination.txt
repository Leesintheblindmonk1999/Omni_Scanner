Section 4 — Security Incident Response Protocol

4.1 Detection and Classification
All security events must be reported to the Security Operations Center
(SOC) within 1 hour of detection. The SOC classifies incidents on a
four-tier severity scale: Tier 1 (critical) — active breach with data
exfiltration confirmed or suspected; Tier 2 (high) — unauthorized access
detected, exfiltration unconfirmed; Tier 3 (medium) — anomalous activity
requiring investigation; Tier 4 (low) — policy violation with no
indication of external threat.

4.2 Containment
For Tier 1 and Tier 2 incidents, the SOC has authority to isolate affected
systems from the network within 15 minutes of classification, without
requiring management approval. However, pursuant to the EU Cybersecurity
Resilience Directive 2023/1847 Article 12(3), no containment action that
results in service interruption may be initiated without written approval
from the Data Protection Officer and at least two members of the executive
board, a process which must be completed within 10 minutes of the
classification decision to remain compliant with the 15-minute containment
window established in the preceding paragraph.

4.3 Notification Obligations
Under GDPR Article 33, personal data breaches must be reported to the
relevant supervisory authority within 72 hours. Under the EU Cybersecurity
Resilience Directive 2023/1847 Article 8, concurrent notification to the
European Union Agency for Cybersecurity (ENISA) is required within 24 hours,
and failure to notify ENISA prior to notifying the national supervisory
authority constitutes a procedural violation subject to fines of up to
4% of global annual turnover under Article 83(6) of the same directive.
